## 1. Complete inventory of LOCKED decisions

### 1.1 Methodology & roles (meta — how we design)
- **Emotion-first 7-step methodology:** Emotion → Philosophy → Mechanic (smallest that works) → Validation (pre-registered, falsifiable) → Constraints → Kill/Defer → Identity Filter ("does this make Point Clear more like itself — its *soul*, not its surface?"). It's a loop; failed validation can send you back to the *emotion*.
- **Roles:** Yoav owns the vision/emotions/decisions (the soul); Claude pressure-tests and may propose in service of testing, never authors the soul. Standing mandate: resistance over agreement.

### 1.2 Emotional hierarchy (the tiebreaker)
**Attachment** (terminal) ← **Identity** ← **Solving problems through your build** (activity) ← **Curiosity** (ignition/engine).

### 1.3 Build identity & freedom
- Finite tree budget; identity from *scarce* investment, not class walls. Archetype = starting *vector*, not a permanent class.
- Respec exists as *costly reinvention*, not a free switch; more forgiving during onboarding.
- Two clocks: fast layer (equipment, weapon-skills, loadouts) / slow layer (finite tree). Experimentation de-risks investment.

### 1.4 Expedition / risk loop
- The **expedition** (a chain of missions/nodes from the hub), not the single mission, is the unit of risk. Loot unsecured until banked. Deeper = more value + more danger; the greed-vs-safety "push or bank?" decision is the core tension. Failure loses unsecured loot; banked gear safe; reduced XP may persist (tuning open).

### 1.5 Combat feel
- Competence baseline; flow → threat → recovery rhythm, tilting to threat with depth. Attrition as a *feeling* (mechanism open). Kinesthetic build legibility (feel your build every few seconds; changes felt in combat, not on a sheet). Wonder (combat creates questions). Enemies threaten through *behavior*, not stats.

### 1.6 Problem-space philosophy
- **The world asks the questions** (enemies + mission + environment + route + depth + modifiers). Deeper = harder through *demand*, not bigger numbers. **Soft counters, never hard gates** (forced by the finite budget). Readability is the **governor on growth** (complexity may grow only as fast as readability keeps pace). Co-op scales through *more problems*, not more HP. Every addition must add a new *question*; every build option must solve problems *differently*, not bigger.

### 1.7 Content model
- **Authored questions, generated answers.** Readable *categories* (agency + veteran build-curiosity) wrap hidden, combinatorially-generated *instances* (curiosity + wonder). "Reveal the category, hide the instance." Curiosity's object migrates world → build×world. Sustained by an *expanding, seasonally-refreshed* primitive vocabulary.

### 1.8 Multiplayer & co-op (Workshop 1)
- Solo and co-op both first-class, from the start.
- **Want, not need:** builds self-sufficient; players *want* each other because together they create experiences that can't exist alone — not because survival requires it.
- **Experience parity** (not reward-neutrality): *"the efficiency gap between solo and co-op must stay small enough that players choose their mode for the experience they want, not because the game pressures them into the more efficient option."* A small, earned co-op delta is fine. Players who *can't* group must still reach all content, build any identity, and enjoy the full journey.
- **No co-op-exclusive content or progression.** Content belongs to all; co-op's value is experiential (stories, interactions, moments).
- **Diversity from identity, not party composition.** The world *validates* identities players already love; it does not *dictate* them. Diversity is seduced, never enforced — four identical builds remain fully viable.
- **Continued emotional validation, not equal validation:** every identity should *periodically feel special*, and none may *permanently* disappear (promise locked; detection mechanism deferred).
- **Division of responsibility:** Build Design creates identities worth loving; the World continually expands the space of meaningful questions (health-aware, not blind — mechanism deferred); Live Service ensures no identity quietly disappears. New questions should reward *committed identities*, not erode them toward generalism.

### 1.9 Onboarding (Workshop 2)
- Bridge emotion = **possibility** (primary), competence (supporting), curiosity (engine), ownership (first seed of attachment).
- **Directional** possibility, not unlimited — honest about the finite budget ("a direction you're excited to become," not "be anything").
- Ownership comes primarily from the **first moment a choice is validated in gameplay**, not from the selection screen.
- Principle: *"Onboarding should never teach the entire game. Its purpose is to convince the player the journey of building their character is worth taking."*
